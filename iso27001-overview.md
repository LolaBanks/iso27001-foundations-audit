# ISO/IEC 27001 Overview

ISO/IEC 27001 is an international standard that specifies requirements
for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving
an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

The standard helps organizations protect information assets through
risk-based security controls.

---

## Purpose of ISO/IEC 27001

- Protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information
- Provide a structured approach to information security
- Support regulatory and customer requirements
- Improve trust with stakeholders

---

## Structure of ISO/IEC 27001

The standard consists of:
- Clauses 4–10 (mandatory requirements)
- Annex A (security controls)

---

## Key Clauses Explained Simply

- **Clause 4 – Context of the Organization**  
  Understand internal and external issues.

- **Clause 5 – Leadership**  
  Management commitment to information security.

- **Clause 6 – Planning**  
  Risk assessment and treatment.

- **Clause 7 – Support**  
  Resources, competence, and awareness.

- **Clause 8 – Operation**  
  Implementation of security controls.

- **Clause 9 – Performance Evaluation**  
  Monitoring, audits, and reviews.

- **Clause 10 – Improvement**  
  Corrective actions and continual improvement.

---

## Annex A Controls

Annex A contains a list of information security controls that organizations
select based on risk.

Not all controls are mandatory — selection depends on risk assessment.

---

## Key Takeaway

ISO 27001 is **risk-based**, not checklist-based.
Controls are implemented to address identified risks.
